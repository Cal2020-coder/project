My name is Oai Tran and I am the creater of this webpage. The goal of this webpage is to share what I learned and hope these materials here will help you in your economics classes. Noticed that this is my personal experiences, which does not speak to all. I just want to share what I known and hope that it would help those that are taking lower-division economics classes.

A little bit about me: I am a transfer student, who graduated with an Economics degree B.A. from University of California - Berekeley (UC-Berekeley or Cal). Before I came to Cal, I had attended a few community colleges such as CoastLine, SaddleBack then Chabot College. While in Community Colleges, I took basic classes and never thought about apply to any University; nonetheless, I did it simply because one of my teacher at Chabot college had no hope in the students and kinda look down on the Economics degree from UC. I was told that I need to talk all the math and CS classes if I was to think about appling. I guess it partly true that having a strong math background and CS would help you. I mean it would help you in anything in life pretty much.

Economics is not hard, what hard is that professors did not break down the materials in a more simplier terms or real life examples. This is extremely true in lower-dicision classes. Where you learn theories and terms that you have no ideas what they are talking about. Here, this webpage. I hope to undo exactly that. I will define the terms, translated to everyday english, give examples, then graph them. I hope this approach will help you understand more about what Economics and be able to understand what you read or see on the news.
